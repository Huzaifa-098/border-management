Platform Architecture

System Overview

The Somalia Border Management System (BMS) is a unified national platform for secure passenger registration, vehicle tracking, biometric verification, and inter-agency coordination at all border and transit checkpoints.

Central Biometric Registry48 CheckpointsAES-256 Encryption
🔍 Search documentation…

System Objectives

  • Centralize all border crossing data across federal states and checkpoints
  • Enable real-time vehicle and passenger tracking with GPS integration
  • Integrate biometric identity verification with central national database
  • Support multi-level admin workflows (City Admin, Super Admin, Officers)
  • Provide immutable audit trails for security and regulatory compliance
  • Enable digital travel permits and driver credential management
  • Automate inter-city vehicle notifications to destination cities
  • Deliver multi-channel alerts (SMS, WhatsApp, Email, in-app)
  • Support emergency response coordination across agencies

Key Benefits

  • Reduced border wait times through pre-registration and QR clearance
  • Automated duplicate and blacklist detection at every checkpoint
  • GPS-based inter-city vehicle monitoring with ETA predictions
  • Unified reporting for federal, state, and city authorities
  • Role-based access ensuring data privacy and least-privilege
  • Cloud-hosted central database with automated daily backups
  • Incident management with photo evidence and escalation paths
  • Driver card and digital travel permit management

Steps 1–5: Registration & Approval

1
Traveler Plans Trip

Traveler determines origin, destination, purpose, and plans origin city, destination city, and travel purpose.

2
Staff Registration

Checkpoint officer captures name, phone, travel purpose, and destination at the checkpoint terminal.

3
Biometric Capture

Fingerprint and facial scan performed; matched against the central biometric registry with duplicate detection.

4
City Admin Review

Flagged cases routed to city admin for registration review and initial approval.

5
Super Admin Approval

Complex cases, policy exceptions and flagged cases escalated to national super admin queue.

Steps 6–9: Clearance & Archive

6
QR Code Receipt

Digital travel permit or driver card generated with unique QR code; sent via SMS/WhatsApp to traveler.

7
Checkpoint Verification

QR scanned at border gate; officer confirms identity, permit validity, and logs clearance event.

8
Central Database Sync

All events synced to cloud central database within 30 seconds; available for inter-checkpoint queries.

9
Reports Generated

Crossing data archived; daily/monthly reports auto-generated for federal and city authorities.
